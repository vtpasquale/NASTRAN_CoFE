PBEAM

Defines the properties of a beam element (CBEAM entry). Tapered beams are not supported.

Format

123456789
PBEAMPIDMIDAI1I2I12JNSM
C1C2D1D2E1E2F1F2
K1K2

Example

PBEAM,1,1,5.065E+10,1.4125E+9,1.4125E+8,0.0,2.1192E+8,1.0952E-6
,,,,,,,,
,1.0,1.0

Fields

Field Contents Default Value
PID Property identification number. (Integer > 0) Required
MID Material identification number. (Integer > 0) Required
A Area of the beam cross section. (Real > 0.0) Required
I1 Area moment of inertia for bending in plane 1 about the neutral axis. See Remark 1. (Real > 0.0) Required
I2 Area moment of inertia for bending in plane 2 about the neutral axis. See Remark 1. (Real > 0.0) Required
I12 Area product of inertia. Must be 0.0 or blank. 0.0
J Torsional stiffness parameter. (Real) Required
NSM Nonstructural mass per unit length. (Real) 0.0
Ci, Di, Ei, Fi The y and z locations (i = 1 corresponds to y and i = 2 corresponds to z) in element coordinates relative to the shear center (see the diagram following the remarks) for stress data recovery. (Real) y = z = 0.0
K1, K2 Shear stiffness factor K in K*A*G for plane 1 and plane 2. See Remark 2. (Real) Required

Remarks:

  1. Figure 1 describes the PBEAM element coordinate system where \begin{align} I1= & I_{(zz)_{elem}} \nonumber \\ I2= & I_{(yy)_{elem}} \nonumber \\ I12 = & I_{(zy)_{elem}} \nonumber \\ J = & I_{(xx)_{elem}} \nonumber \end{align}
  2. The shear stiffness factors K1 and K2 adjust the effective transverse shear cross-section area according to the Timoshenko beam theory. Nominal values of 1.0 approximate the effects of shear deformation. Look up values for your specific cross section. To neglect shear deformation (i.e., to obtain the Bernoulli-Euler beam theory), the values of K1 and K2 should be set to 0.0.
  3. Taper is not supported.
  4. Warping torsion is not supported.
  5. Nonzero I12 is not supported.
